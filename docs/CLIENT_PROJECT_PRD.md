# VortexOps Project Brief

Prepared for: **Vortex Breaks**  
Project: **VortexOps**

---

## 1. Purpose

VortexOps is intended to provide Vortex Breaks with a more structured operational foundation by reducing reliance on disconnected spreadsheets, manual tracking, and fragmented internal processes.

The purpose of this project is to bring important day-to-day workflows into one cohesive system so the business can operate with greater clarity, consistency, and confidence.

At a high level, this initiative is focused on helping Vortex Breaks:

- improve visibility across inventory and operations
- reduce manual reconciliation and follow-up work
- create cleaner review and approval processes
- improve trust in operational and financial outputs
- establish a stronger platform for future growth

---

## 2. Business context

Vortex Breaks operates within a workflow that depends on inventory handling, live shows, post-show reconciliation, payout preparation, and ongoing internal communication.

As the business grows, these workflows become more difficult to manage when information is spread across multiple spreadsheets, handwritten notes, screenshots, chat threads, and partial systems.

This project exists to reduce that fragmentation and replace it with a more unified and operationally reliable process.

---

## 3. Core challenge

Based on the current understanding of the business, several operational pain points are creating unnecessary friction:

- important information lives in too many places
- manual data entry exists across multiple steps
- inventory movement is not always easy to trace
- post-show processes require too much manual review and follow-up
- payout preparation can be time-consuming to validate
- internal feedback and implementation notes are not always captured in a structured way

These issues increase the likelihood of:

- slower operations
- avoidable mistakes
- unclear ownership of changes
- inconsistent records
- difficulty scaling cleanly as activity increases

VortexOps is intended to address these issues by centralizing the operating process and making workflows easier to follow, review, and trust.

---

## 4. Project objective

The objective of VortexOps is to create a more dependable internal operating environment for Vortex Breaks.

Rather than relying on separate tools for separate steps, the project is meant to support a more connected flow across:

- inventory handling
- operational review
- reconciliation workflows
- payout preparation
- internal feedback and collaboration

The broader aim is to improve operational accuracy, reduce administrative overhead, and make the business easier to manage over time.

---

## 5. What success looks like

A successful outcome for this project would mean Vortex Breaks has:

- less dependence on spreadsheets for everyday operations
- better visibility into inventory and operational status
- clearer review and approval steps
- stronger internal accountability
- more reliable operational records
- a stronger base for future reporting and automation

Success is not simply having software in place. Success is having a system that makes the business easier to run.

---

## 6. Operational focus

Based on the current project direction, VortexOps is intended to support several closely related areas of the business:

- inventory visibility and movement
- post-show operational review
- deduction and reconciliation workflows
- payout preparation
- internal review, annotations, and feedback capture

These areas are interconnected, which is why the project is being approached as one operational platform rather than a collection of separate tools.

---

## 7. Inventory direction

One of the most important goals of this project is improving how inventory is handled operationally.

The direction is not only to know how many units exist, but to create a clearer operational trail around how inventory enters the business, how it is organized, and where it ultimately goes.

This includes moving toward a more realistic inventory process such as:

- structured receiving
- grouped inventory handling such as pallets, cases, boxes, or smaller units
- clearer visibility into storage and assignment
- cleaner movement from intake through final location

This matters because it aligns the system more closely with how inventory is actually handled in the real world, rather than treating inventory only as a static count.

---

## 8. Purchasing and intake opportunity

One of the clearest future opportunities within this project is improving the purchasing-to-inventory process.

Based on what is currently understood, inventory purchasing may involve a mix of:

- distributors or suppliers
- purchase confirmations
- invoices or receipts
- shipping costs
- other related fees
- manual entry into inventory records

That process creates additional friction before purchased inventory becomes usable in the operating workflow.

A more mature version of this initiative should support a cleaner intake process where purchased inventory can be tracked more directly from supplier receipt into operational inventory handling.

The business value of that shift would include:

- better cost visibility
- cleaner intake processes
- stronger auditability
- better long-term reporting quality

---

## 9. Cost visibility direction

Another important goal of the project is improving visibility into inventory-related cost.

Based on the current understanding, the business would likely benefit from better tracking of:

- purchase cost
- shipping cost
- additional associated fees
- landed cost
- cost variation when similar inventory is acquired at different prices over time

This is important because inventory is not only a quantity issue. It also affects decision-making, financial visibility, and future reporting quality.

---

## 10. Long-term considerations

Several opportunities are already visible, even if they are not immediate priorities.

These may include:

- scanner-supported workflows
- more structured purchasing intake
- stronger cost reporting
- deeper operational reporting
- time tracking or workforce-related features later on

These are not the primary focus of the immediate project goal, but they represent logical extensions of the platform as the business matures.

## 11. Project roadmap view

The project can be understood as a staged operational rollout, with each phase building on the last.

| Timeline | Primary focus | Intended outcome |
|---|---|---|
| `Week 1` | Operational discovery, spreadsheet review, workflow mapping, reporting analysis, platform architecture planning, and operational process evaluation | Confirm how the business currently operates and define the right structure before core buildout continues |
| `Weeks 2-6` | Inventory workflows, stream tracking systems, dashboard foundations, reporting foundations, seller management workflows, and centralized operational tooling | Establish the first practical internal operating foundation and begin making the platform meaningfully usable |
| `Weeks 6-12` | Reconciliation workflows, payout preparation systems, operational reporting enhancements, administrative tooling, workflow refinement, platform stabilization, and optimization improvements | Move the platform from core structure into a more dependable day-to-day operations system |
| `Months 3-4` | Workflow automation systems, process streamlining, advanced reporting enhancements, notification systems, operational efficiency improvements, and internal workflow optimization | Improve speed, reduce manual follow-up, and strengthen the usefulness of the platform beyond its initial baseline |
| `Months 4-6` | Platform maturity improvements, operational scaling enhancements, integration expansion, automation refinement, advanced tooling, reporting evolution, and long-term operational growth initiatives | Support broader maturity, deeper operational capability, and better readiness for continued business growth |
| `Ongoing` | Future enhancements, additional operational projects, optimization improvements, and long-term partnership support as business needs evolve | Continue maturing the platform alongside the business over time |

### Initial usability expectation

From a practical standpoint, the platform should begin becoming internally usable during the **Weeks 2-6** window as the core operational foundation is established.

Its reliability and day-to-day usefulness should continue to strengthen during the **Weeks 6-12** period as reconciliation, reporting, administrative tooling, and refinement work are layered in.

This roadmap view is intended to reflect the overall implementation progression and operational maturity path for the project.

---

## 12. Closing summary

VortexOps is intended to help Vortex Breaks move from fragmented operational tracking to a more unified, scalable, and reliable way of running the business.

The immediate value of the project is better structure around inventory, reconciliation, payouts, and internal review workflows.

The longer-term value is that it creates a foundation for more mature purchasing, intake, reporting, and operational automation as the business continues to grow.
